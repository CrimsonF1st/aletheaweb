Here, The Dead Walk

Stretching along the eastern coast of the Shimmering Sea is Ka'duatep, the Undying Empire. Headed by a supremely powerful necromancer, it is a recent player to the stage of Reclith. Approximately three centuries ago, this necromancer led an army of the undead to war against the multitude of small kingdoms in that part of the world. Over the course of fifty years, his conquests ceased and what became clear was that his expansion had been made possible by an aura of undeath that covered the territory he now claimed. Every mortal creature, upon their death in this aura, rises again a day later under the control of the Undying Emperor.

Real World Influences

Ka'duatep is the mostly obviously inspired of these regions. It leans heavily on the cultures, architecture, and languages of Ancient Egypt - through a very obviously fantastical lense. Some parts of the Undying Empire borrow as well from the Achaemenid Persians, including styles of dress and some political structures. Overall, Ka'duatep exists across a very similar geographic space as parts of Egypt, with lush river cities and dry deserts surrounding them.

Notable Locations

Phindisema
The capital city of the Empire. It surrounds the circular palace of the Emperor in neat concentric rings. Veritable armies of undead labourers work tirelessly to expand the city upwards and outwards.
Nedjumt
Called the Osseous Citadel, it is a fortress in the north of the Empire that guards the border with Levidia. It's walls are made entirely out of bones, although it is unknown where these bones originated.
Ahktnunis
A necropolis in the deep desert. Few beyond the borders of Ka'duatep know who or what is kept within this tomb-city, but it guarded by fierce warriors who excel in the arts of bone and spirit magic.
Dusht
One of the few pockets of resistance within the aura of undeath. Dusht is a small mountain city far to the east and manages to survive through the efforts of the Duskers. These specially trained mages track down the recent dead so they can prevent them from rising again.

Culture and Religion

Little is known about the day-to-day life of the citizens of the Undying Empire to those outside its borders. What is known is that most of the physical labour and military demands of the Empire are filled by the undead, and that these workers follow the orders of varying levels of necromancer-priests.

What is said in whispered words around the fire at night, or while looking at the cold light of the moons, is that the great hordes of zombies outnumber the living ten to one, and that they are given regular sacrifices of living flesh to devour, and that the Emperor can see every action and hear every words through the dead eyes and dead ears of his servents. There is no evidence for these claims.

Religion in the Empire is varied. The official religion of the Empire worships the god of the dead - whose name has long been lost to time, and who is said by the rest of the world to have perished in the war against the Archenemy - but individuals are allowed to practice whatever beliefs they like. As a consequence, most people are very familiar with the Faith of the Dead and often practice some of its rituals alongside those of whichever god or pantheon they prefer.
